How to Help

Now it’s time to learn what YOU can do to help our sweet bee friends in your own home!

1. Plant a bee garden

Try growing bee-friendly plants in your garden! Native flowers are best — we recommend Black-eyed Susans and Purple Coneflowers.

2. Avoid using pesticides

Pesticides can be incredibly harmful for bees, as it can upset their sensitive bodies! Try removing the pesticides from your garden — the bees will thank you!

3. Make a bee bath

Fill a shallow bowl with fresh water and place pebbles and rocks inside that break the surface. That way, thirsty bees can stop by for a drink!

4. Support local beekeepers

Local beekeepers in our area work hard to nurture our bee friends. Buy honey and other bee products from them to show your support!

5. Don't hurt the bees!

Next time you see a bee nearby — don’t swat it! Our bee friends are just doing their duties to the ecosystem. Leave them “bee!”

6. Give bees a sugary snack

A tired bee loves a boost of sugar! Mix two teaspoons of granulated sugar with one teaspoon of water and drop it on a plate or flower for a bee’s quick pick-me-up!

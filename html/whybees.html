Why bees?

So, what's the big deal about bees anyways?

Well, bees pollinate around one-third of the entire world’s food supply — and that means that the food you had for breakfast today probably came from them!

Sadly, bees are quickly going extinct. Scientists have estimated that 1 in 6 bee species are regionally extinct,
and over 40% are in danger of extinction.

How did this happen?

Bees are mostly at risk of endangerment because of human activities. Big changes in the use of land, usage of bee-harming pesticides, and even climate change have all had a huge effect on the bee population.

All of these factors have reduced the food supply for bees, damaged their habitat, and even hurting the bees themselves. That’s why we’re here!
